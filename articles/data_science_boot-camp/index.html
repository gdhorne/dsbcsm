Welcome recruits!

The Data Science Specialization offered by Johns Hopkins University is a challenging nine-course sequence focusing on the fundamentals of data science. Success requires a strategy. The purpose of this blog to equip each of you with the knowledge and skills to complete the specialisation.

According to Jeff Leek the keyword in "data science" is science not data. An article in Financial Times posed the question "Big data: are we making a big mistake?"1, arguing for a rigorous methodology. An article "The Data Science Methology", published on Data Science 101, argues for adoption of the scientific method familiar to scientists in the natural sciences. 2

Data Science Methodology

  1. problem formulation (hypothesis)
  2. obtain data (experiment)
  3. analysis (validate or refute hypothesis)
  4. data product (report)

The courses in the Johns Hopkins University Data Science Specialization "emphasise a data science methodology rather than focusing primarily on data science technique. The method is not dependent upon the dataset size; it scales from small data to big data. The data science method equates to the scientific method used in the natural sciences. To this end, the focus of the ten-course series including a capstone project is to provide the learner with:

  • an introduction to the key ideas behind reproducible research,
  • an introduction to the tools and techniques to transform raw data into a presentable report,
  • an opportunity to gain hands-on practice, and
  • an appreciation of the mathematics & statistics involved in data science.

[T]he instructors have taken care throughout to demonstrate a responsible, scientifically-based approach to collecting, curating and analyzing data sources," says specialisation participant John Frederick Thiels.

Core Courses

The courses comprising the Data Science Specialization are:

  1. Data Scientist's Toolbox
  2. R Programming
  3. Getting and Cleaning Data
  4. Exploratory Data Analysis
  5. Reproducible Research
  6. Statistical Inference
  7. Regression Models
  8. Practical Machine Learning
  9. Developing Data Products

These courses taught by Brian Caffo, Jeff Leek, and Roger D. Peng enable the learner to get the foundational skills. While the lectures and assignments build these foundational skills, learners often required further explanations. The course forums allow learners to discuss the lecture topics and assignments. As a Community Teaching Assistant (CTA) it became evident a companion guide would be beneficial. Eventually the instructors released a series of e-books which began as compendiums of the existing lecture notes and now provide further coverage of the topics.

Capstone Project Course

If you have completed all of the core courses on Signature Track, you will be eligible to register for the capstone project course.

Structure of the Boot-Camp Survival Manual

Boot-camp is structured as a set of related topics each covering one or more of the core courses. A tutorial-style emphasising practical application enables recruits to learn the work-flow typically practiced throughout a data analysis project.

Course Dependency and Recommended Sequence

Although the courses are standalone, the knowledge is cumulative. The pedagogical course dependencies are available from Johns Hopkins University.

    Figure 1 Course dependency diagram (Jeff Jetton: created July 2014; used with permission)

You need a language or system to perform the tasks (R Programming) and data to analyse (Getting and Cleaning Data) to get a sense of the data (Exploratory Data Analysis) before building models and drawing inferences (Statistical Inference, Regression Models) or making predictions (Practical Machine Learning) from the data before presenting your conclusions and supporting evidence (Building Data Products, Reproducible Research).

The recommended mathematics background is linear algebra and introductory statistics (descriptive and inferential). Statistical Inference and Regression Models, courses in this specialisation, cover all the basic statistical concepts forming a solid foundation for subsequent courses in the Data Science Specialization. These courses along with Practical Machine Learning are the theoretical underpinnings, while the other six courses are applied in nature: obtaininng data, scrubbing data, exploring data, modeling data, and interpreting data collectively known as the OSEMN (prounounced as awesome) model.
